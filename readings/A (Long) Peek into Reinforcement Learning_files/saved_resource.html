                                                                                nbro a year ago edited

                                                                                You say "Each state is associated with a value function (V(s)) predicting the expected amount of future rewards we are able to receive in this state". This is misleading. V(s) is not the total future cumulative (and discounted) reward you will obtain in state s, but the total future cumulative (and discounted) reward that you obtain if you are in s and and then follow the "corresponding" policy. In other words, if you stay in the same state and don't take any actions, you usually don't get any rewards. V(s) represents what you predict you will get in the future given that you're in state s and then follow the "corresponding" policy. So, V(s) is not just the reward you obtain e.g. if you end up in state s!
